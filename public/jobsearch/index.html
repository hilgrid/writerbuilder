How to Get Noticed by PM Hiring Managers in 2026

7 AI tools built from a Maven Lightning Lesson with Ben Erez, Nickey Skarstad, and Hilary Gridley

The PM job market in 2026 is brutal. AI-generated applications have flooded every inbox. These tools help you break through the noise, build proof of work, and write outreach that actually gets responses.

The Journey

Work through these three phases to stand out from the flood of AI-generated applications.

1

Prepare Your Story

Before you apply anywhere, get clear on your narrative and where to focus your energy.

  • Job Fit Reality Check
  • Least Charitable Read Strategist
2

Build Your Evidence

In 2026, everyone has a polished resume. Stand out by making something.

  • Proof of Work Builder
  • Spearfishing Demo Planner
3

Make Contact

Get your pitch in front of the right person — cold, warm, or through a referral.

  • Cold Outreach Subject Line Generator
  • Warm Intro Request Coach
  • Forwardable Email Coach

The Tools

Each tool is a Custom GPT you can use for free. Click to open in ChatGPT.

1
Phase 1: Prepare

Job Fit Reality Check

Before you spend 5 hours on an application, make sure it's worth it. This tool gives you a quick gut-check on whether a role is a strong fit, a stretch, or a waste of time.

Use this when: You're deciding where to focus your energy.

2
Phase 1: Prepare

Least Charitable Read Strategist

Hiring managers pattern-match fast. If your background doesn't obviously fit, they'll make assumptions — usually unflattering ones. This tool helps you identify those assumptions and craft a counter-narrative.

Use this when: You're switching domains, levels, or company types.
Use tool →
3
Phase 2: Build

Proof of Work Builder

Create a thinking artifact that shows how your mind works: a competitive analysis, product teardown, strategy memo, or user research synthesis. This tool helps you pick the right format and scope it to 3-5 hours.

Use this when: You want to demonstrate strategic thinking without building a full prototype.
Use tool →
4
Phase 2: Build

Spearfishing Demo Planner

Build a functional prototype or clickable demo that uses the company's product, APIs, or problem space. This tool helps you plan something you can ship in about a day — for roles you really want.

Use this when: It's a dream role and you want to show you can actually build, not just analyze.

Based on the spearfishing framework from Fractal Bootcamp.

5
Phase 3: Contact

Cold Outreach Subject Line Generator

Most cold outreach fails at the subject line. "Interest in [Company]" gets ignored. This tool helps you write subject lines that are offers of value, not asks for time.

Use this when: You're emailing someone cold and need to get opened.
Use tool →
6
Phase 3: Contact

Warm Intro Request Coach

Warm intros are still the most effective way in. But asking wrong can be awkward — or damage relationships. This tool helps you make the ask the right way.

Use this when: You have a mutual connection and want to do it right.
Use tool →
7
Phase 3: Contact

Forwardable Email Coach

The final step: write an email your mutual connection can forward with one click. This tool walks you through strategy, specifics, and drafting — then helps you find an edge that makes your email impossible to ignore.

Use this when: You're ready to write the actual outreach email.
